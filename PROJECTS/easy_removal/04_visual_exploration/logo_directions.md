The truck should feel like a practical local service vehicle, not a logistics fleet, courier van or large moving truck. The mark should suggest movement through a slight forward lean, wheel alignment or simplified motion line.

### Construction logic
- Build the truck on an 8-unit grid.
- Use a stable horizontal structure.
- Keep the cab and rear load section as simple geometric blocks.
- Use slightly rounded corners across the cab, load bed and wheels.
- Show the rear as open, caged or lightly framed.
- Keep wheel detail minimal.
- Remove internal cage lines at small sizes.
- Pair the icon with a strong, simple wordmark.
- Use #5C8165 as the main mark colour.
- Use #202A30 for the wordmark.
- Use #C39B46 only as a small accent, such as a motion cue or load marker.

### Why it fits the brand
This is the most immediately understandable route.

It clearly communicates:
- A truck-based service
- Local collection
- Practical removal
- Small-load jobs
- Fast response

It works well for a business that needs instant recognition in WhatsApp chats, flyers, community groups and vehicle signage.

### Where it may fail
This direction can become generic quickly.

Main risks:
- Looks like a moving company.
- Looks like a courier service.
- Looks too corporate if the truck is too polished.
- Looks too childish if the truck is over-rounded.
- Loses distinction if the caged back is not clear.

The mark needs restraint. Too much detail will weaken it at small sizes.

### Best usage context
Best for:
- Truck signage
- Flyers
- WhatsApp profile image
- Facebook and neighbourhood ads
- Local service cards
- Site boards
- Quick quote templates

## 02. Load-box direction

### Concept
A compact open load-box or cube mark representing the 4-cubic-metre removal load.

This direction focuses on the load itself rather than the vehicle. The icon should suggest a small, manageable amount of refuse or household junk being collected and cleared.

### Construction logic
- Build the load-box on an 8-unit grid.
- Use a simple cube, open container or framed box form.
- Keep the shape compact, not skip-like.
- Use rounded corners to keep it approachable.
- Add a slight forward motion cue through an arrow, offset edge or angled base.
- Avoid parcel-box styling.
- Avoid making it look like storage, courier delivery or packaging.
- Use consistent stroke width across all box lines.
- Remove inner construction lines at small sizes.
- Pair with a bold, practical wordmark.

### Why it fits the brand
This direction is more ownable than a standard truck.

It communicates:
- Small loads
- Simple removals
- Contained mess
- Quick collection
- Practical job size

It also gives the brand a scalable icon system if Easy Removal later adds categories, such as garden refuse, garage clear-outs or household junk.

### Where it may fail
The load-box may be misread as:
- A courier brand
- A storage company
- A packaging supplier
- A moving company

To avoid this, the box must feel open, service-based and removal-focused. It should not look like a sealed parcel.

### Best usage context
Best for:
- Icon-only mark
- Social profile images
- Service category icons
- Stickers
- Quote forms
- Website cards
- Uniform badges
- Reusable brand system elements

### Required output notes
- Primary horizontal logo: load-box mark left, wordmark right.
- Stacked logo: load-box above wordmark.
- Icon-only mark: simplified open cube or framed load symbol.
- One-colour version: solid green or charcoal.
- Reversed version: white mark on #5C8165 or #202A30.
- WhatsApp profile version: centred load-box with thickened strokes.
- Truck signage version: wordmark dominant, load-box used as a secondary identifier.



## 03. Cleared-space direction

### Concept
A negative-space mark showing an area being cleared.

The logo should focus on the result: clutter removed, space opened and the job done. This direction should feel practical rather than abstract. The viewer should understand that something has been taken away.

### Construction logic
- Build the symbol on an 8-unit grid.
- Start with a solid block, pile, box or rectangular area.
- Cut out a clean negative-space path, opening or arrow.
- Use the cleared space as the main visual idea.
- Keep the silhouette bold.
- Avoid fine internal shapes.
- Use rounded corners to stay consistent with the brand.
- Keep the wordmark simple and strong to support comprehension.
- Use green for the filled shape.
- Use white or negative space for the cleared area.
- Use ochre only if it helps show the action of clearing.

### Why it fits the brand
This is the most benefit-led direction.

It communicates:
- The mess is gone.
- The space is clear.
- The service solves the problem.
- The process is simple.
- The result is immediate.

It has the strongest potential to become a distinctive brand asset if executed cleanly.

### Where it may fail
This route is less literal.

Main risks:
- Too abstract for a local service business.
- Could look like cleaning, landscaping or storage.
- May need the name beside it to make sense.
- May not be clear enough on first glance.
- Could weaken in WhatsApp profile size if the negative space is too subtle.

This direction needs strong testing at small sizes before approval.

### Best usage context
Best for:
- Brand identity refinement
- Website and digital use
- Before-and-after posts
- Quote documents
- Social templates
- Business cards
- More polished client-facing materials

### Required output notes
- Primary horizontal logo: negative-space mark left, wordmark right.
- Stacked logo: cleared-space symbol above wordmark.
- Icon-only mark: bold block with clear cut-out.
- One-colour version: solid mark with negative-space cut-out.
- Reversed version: white mark on charcoal or green.
- WhatsApp profile version: simplified cut-out with high contrast.
- Truck signage version: use only if the symbol remains instantly readable from distance.



## Recommended first exploration

Start with the Truck-led direction.

It is the most commercially direct and easiest for the market to understand.

Then test the Load-box direction, because it may offer a stronger long-term identity system.

Use the Cleared-space direction as the more strategic route, but only keep it if it remains readable without explanation.

## Evaluation criteria

Each direction should be judged against:

- Is it clear at 24 px?
- Does it avoid looking like skip hire?
- Does it avoid looking like a furniture moving company?
- Does it feel local and practical?
- Can it work on a truck door?
- Can it work as a WhatsApp profile image?
- Is the wordmark readable at speed?
- Does the mark still work in one colour?
- Can the system scale into flyers, quotes and service icons?
